Galeria

J. Robert Oppenheimer was an American theoretical physicist best known for his role as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project during World War II. Under his leadership, a team of brilliant scientists developed the first atomic bomb, a breakthrough that dramatically changed the course of global conflict and modern warfare. Oppenheimer’s intellect, organizational skill, and ability to unite diverse researchers made him one of the most influential scientific figures of the 20th century.

J. Robert Oppenheimer, often called the “father of the atomic bomb,” was a brilliant physicist whose work reshaped both science and world history. Born in 1904 in New York City, he excelled academically from an early age and later became a leading figure in quantum physics. His deep curiosity and ability to explain complex ideas made him a respected professor and researcher long before his involvement in wartime science. By the early 1940s, his expertise and leadership had placed him at the center of one of the most ambitious scientific projects ever attempted.
